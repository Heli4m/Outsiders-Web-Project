Psychological and Physical Effects of Gang Violence on Adolsecents

A Website By: Kevin, Cherry, Bin, Bim and Liam

Scars of Tulsa

When we read the Outsiders, we generally think of a rumble as a physical clash between two rival groups. However, the true damage of gang violence isn't found in the bruises, but found in the psychological restructuring of a child's mind.

In the mid 1960s, author S.E.Hinton explored a cycle of trauma surrounding the issues of gang violence, especially on a group of teenagers/young adults. Through various different characters, the author does a great job at explaining and showcasing the horrors of gang violence and what it can do to an adolescent.

From Fiction to Reality

This website connects the fictional experiences of our beloved Greasers to modern day articles and socio-psychological research, with the purpose of showing you the fact that the problem is still rampant to this day. By comparing a work of literature in the 1960s to studies nowadays, we have uncovered a chilling truth: even though the weapons in gang violence have changed and evolved, the "fight or flight" instincts and the psychological changes remain the same within teenagers.

Explore the files below to see how violence rewires the psychological structure of a young adult.
